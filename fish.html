Answer:

The key theme of "Fish is Fish" by Leo Lionni is the idea of transformation and acceptance of oneself and others. The story follows the journey of two best friends, a fish and a tadpole, as they explore the world around them. When the tadpole turns into a frog and leaves the water, the fish is left behind and wonders what the world is like on land. The frog returns to the water and describes the new things he has seen, but the fish can only imagine them.

The fish becomes so curious about the world outside of water that he decides to leave and explore it for himself. He is fascinated by the different animals he sees, but when he tries to imitate them, he finds that he cannot do what they can do. He eventually realizes that he is a fish and that he cannot change who he is. He returns to the water, grateful for what he has and accepting of who he is.

The theme of transformation is highlighted throughout the story as the characters change and adapt to their environment. However, the ultimate message is one of self-acceptance and being content with who you are, even if you cannot do everything that others can do.
